# The Duck — Origin & Lore

---

## What the Duck Is

The duck is the player character — a 2D side-scroller entity with the following confirmed traits:
- Made of **Black Matter**, possibly stabilized with a golden/yellow material that locks BM form without continuous observation
- Contains a **transferred human consciousness** (most likely a scientist's)
- **Immortal** — dies and respawns, time is not rewound, consciousness persists
- Has **three tongues** used to grab sticky surfaces (gloamvine flowers, etc.)

---

## Most Likely Canon: Origin

The duck was artificially created during the quarantine period by infected team members. A scientist — partially infected and facing full corruption — chose to transfer their consciousness into a Black Matter construct before losing their human form entirely. The duck's yellow/gold coloring may reflect the stabilizing material used to lock the BM form in place.

**Why a duck specifically:** Unclear. Candidates:
- Simplest available animal on the island at the time
- Scientists chose a low-intelligence animal as the vessel
- The duck imitates things — the scientist's consciousness shaped the form somehow
- **TBD**

**Three tongues:** Likely a Black Matter modification artifact. No confirmed explanation. **TBD.**

## The Pofinho Theory

**Pofinho** is the scientist most likely to be the consciousness inside the duck. Evidence:
- Authored the satellite log about BM expanding on the island surface — one of the last known logs, suggesting he was still active late in the timeline
- His name appears in design notes as the leading candidate for the consciousness transfer
- No confirmed survival or exit from the island

This is unconfirmed in-game. Treat as the working canon until contradicted.

## Endgame Candidate

The duck reaches a room with a scientist in a chair, brain-computer interface active, a giant screen showing a live feed of exactly what the duck sees — a recursive loop. The scientist says **"Unplug."** Screen goes black. Duck falls. Credits or final sequence.

Implication: the duck's consciousness was never truly free — it was always being observed by the scientist, mirroring the game's own observation mechanic turned inward.
