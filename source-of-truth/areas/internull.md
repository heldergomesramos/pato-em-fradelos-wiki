# Internull & The Stranger — The Other Dimension

> "Internull" is the name the scientists gave to the hypothetical dimension where BM goes when unobserved. By the end of the game, a perceptive player realizes the scientists were describing The Stranger's interior all along — they just didn't know it. The name appears in early logs and becomes quiet dramatic irony.

---

## Teleporter Spheres

Large black spheres found in the Energy Facility (and potentially elsewhere). Mechanism:
- Enter the sphere
- Input coordinates (5×5 binary grid, each cell ON or OFF)
- Exit from another sphere at the destination

**Interior:** Completely black. Standing inside means you cannot see out, and outside observers cannot see in — the exact conditions for BM's vanishing principle.

**How teleportation works:** Teleporter spheres work by briefly routing the traveler *through* The Stranger — using it as a shortcut through a dimension where time moves differently, which is why teleportation is instant across large distances. The traveler exists inside The Stranger for a fraction of a second, then re-emerges at the destination portal.

**Why it needs the generator:** The BM generator provides the energy required to punch through into The Stranger and pull back out on the other side. Without it active, the portals can't complete the transit.

The Depths Chamber is accessed by a creative use of a Teleporter Sphere's mechanics — using momentum and recalling to slam through the Aquarium glass. → See `areas/depths.md`

## Entering The Stranger via Sleep

When you sleep mid-transit through a Teleporter Sphere, you stop being a conscious observer. The teleporter system loses its anchor — a conscious entity is what allows the portal to complete the return trip, because consciousness is what BM responds to. Without it, the system can't pull you back. You remain inside The Stranger, drifting, until you wake up.

You didn't hack the system. You became temporarily indistinguishable from the unobserved BM around you. This reinforces Pofinho's nature — he is BM with consciousness, so sleeping makes him briefly *become* the environment.

**Mechanic:** Sleep to enter, sleep to exit. Symmetrical and learnable without tutorialing.

- Sleep inside an active teleporter sphere → wake up inside The Stranger
- The duck is suspended motionless inside the sphere from any outside perspective
- Explore The Stranger for as long as desired
- Sleep again → wake at the intended destination as if transit completed normally
- **No time passes outside** — literally zero time, regardless of time spent inside. Consistent with The Stranger's time properties.

**Teaching the exit:** The first Stranger zone is almost completely empty and very short. The player arrives, finds little, and needs to figure out how to leave. Having just slept to get in, sleeping to get out is a natural experiment. The discovery feels like a personal win rather than a tutorial.

## The Two-Axis Destination System

Two independent variables control where you end up when sleeping during teleportation:

**Axis 1 — Portal coordinates (5×5 grid):** Each teleporter sphere has a fixed coordinate set on a 5×5 binary grid. This determines *which zone* of The Stranger's interior you arrive in. Different portals always lead to their assigned region. The grid is inputted physically at the sphere — players can input custom coordinates to access regions not mapped to any known portal.

**Axis 2 — The Stranger's orbital position:** Controls *depth* within the assigned zone. Normal position = shallow arrival, accessible areas only. Zenith (midnight / eclipse) = deepest penetration into that zone, revealing areas unreachable at other times.

Each portal unlocks a new Stranger region. The eclipse lets you go deeper in *any* region, rewarding revisits. The core of The Stranger requires both the correct coordinates *and* eclipse timing — two independent keys.

## The Hidden Deep Portal

A portal exists deep underground — in The Depths, where BM concentration is highest and oldest. Its coordinates are not in any scientist's published logs. Finding them requires:

1. Learning that custom coordinates are possible (from teleporter research logs)
2. Finding partial coordinates in The Stranger itself — a frozen screen in a mid-game zone, meaning you had to already be inside to get the clue to go deeper
3. Finding the other half in PE's Stranger research in the lab — only interpretable with enough context

**The coordinates are hidden in the walls of a specific room.** Engraved but invisible in normal light. Throwing the Gloamvine Core into the room illuminates them — the player must actively light the space. Neither half alone means anything. Together they point to the deep portal.

Sleeping in the deep portal during the eclipse at midnight sends Pofinho to the core of The Stranger.

PE built this portal from the outside, choosing the Depths because BM concentration there is highest and oldest — the membrane between this world and The Stranger is thinnest there.

## The Stranger — Interior Properties

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Time | Behaves differently — may not pass at all without consciousness to observe it |
| Access | Teleporter Spheres + Sleep; deep portal during eclipse for the core |
| Traversal | Conscious beings (Pofinho, The Observer) can move through it |
| Recording | A camera sent through recorded nothing — no time passed inside during its transit |
| Frozen states | Objects exist in multiple observed states simultaneously — mid-motion preserved |

**Theory on time:** Time in The Stranger requires observation/consciousness to "render." A camera is not conscious, so no time passed for it. Pofinho is conscious, so he can traverse meaningfully.
