# Lore — Consciousness Statues

---

## What They Are

Structures built by the research team using **stabilizer ore** from the mines. Each statue contains a fragment of **Pofinho's consciousness** — a piece of who he was, preserved before the main transfer into the duck body.

They are not monuments. They are not decorative. They are a backup system built by two scientists under extreme pressure, knowing the transfer might not carry everything across intact.

---

## Why They Were Built

Transferring a human consciousness into a BM construct was unknown territory. No one had done it successfully. The risk was that the consciousness would arrive fragmented, incomplete, or not arrive at all.

Pofinho and PE's solution: **distribute the non-essential parts of Pofinho's consciousness across multiple statues before the main transfer.** The core — enough to survive, move, act — would make the jump into the duck. The rest would wait in the statues until Pofinho found them.

This is why Pofinho wakes up not knowing who he is. The transfer worked. The core survived. But the memories, the personality, the full shape of who Pofinho was — those are in the statues, waiting.

---

## How They Work

Each statue is made of stabilizer ore — the same material that locks Pofinho's BM form. When Pofinho interacts with a statue, the fragment inside recognizes the consciousness it came from and reintegrates.

The player experiences this as memory — a flash of who Pofinho was. No narration. No text box explaining what happened. Just a moment of something returning.

The statues do not glow or signal their importance visually from a distance. They are identifiable by their material — the same gold ore color as Pofinho's own edges, as the checkpoint borders. A player who is paying attention will recognize what they are before the game explains it.

---

## Distribution

Statues are distributed across the island and the lab — wherever Pofinho and PE had access before the transfer. They are not hidden as puzzles. They are placed where a person working under time pressure would place them: in rooms they used, along paths they knew.

---

## What the Fragments Contain

Each fragment is a piece of who Pofinho was as a human. Together they build a portrait:
- How he worked (always distance first, then detail — PE references this in the final log)
- What he thought about the research
- What he thought about the team
- Something personal and small — not dramatic, just human

The fragments do not need to be delivered in order. The player assembles the picture non-linearly, the way memory actually works.

---

## Lore Significance

The statues answer the question the island quietly asks: *who was the person before he became this?*

By the time Pofinho reaches the observatory and reads PE's final log — which uses his name for the first time — the player has enough fragments to feel the weight of that name. It is not a revelation that comes from nowhere. It lands because the player already knows who Pofinho was.
