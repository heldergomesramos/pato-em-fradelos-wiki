# The Duck — Origin & Lore

---

## What the Duck Is

The duck is the player character — a 2D side-scroller entity with the following confirmed traits:
- Made of **Black Matter**, stabilized with **stabilizer ore** that locks its BM form without continuous active observation
- Contains the **transferred consciousness of Pofinho** — a scientist from the research team
- **Immortal** — dies and respawns; time is not rewound; consciousness persists
- Has **three tongues** used to grab sticky surfaces (gloamvine flowers, etc.) — BM modification artifact, reason TBD
- A **self-observing BM entity** — the only one of his kind

---

## Pofinho — Confirmed Canon

**Pofinho** is the scientist whose consciousness is inside the duck. This is working canon.

Pofinho was the field researcher — direct BM contact, first infected. Rather than evacuating, he used the time to study BM from the inside, becoming the team's most valuable researcher on consciousness and BM integration because he was living it. As full corruption approached, he and PE worked together on a solution.

Pofinho figured out that consciousness and BM could integrate, that the stabilizer ore could lock a form, and built the duck body — a low-intelligence vessel available on the island — and transferred his consciousness into it before corruption was complete.

**Why a duck:**
- **Practical reason:** Ducks were on the island near the lake. Simple, available vessel during a crisis with no time to be selective.
- **Emotional reason (implicit, no dialogue needed):** Pofinho spent his career watching things from a distance — satellite observation, remote documentation. He chose a body built to *move through* the world, not observe it from outside. A quiet character beat about who he was, readable without any text.

**Three tongues:** BM modification artifact. No confirmed explanation. **TBD.**

## The Consciousness Transfer — How It Was Done

Transferring a human consciousness intact into a BM construct was unknown territory. Nobody knew if the consciousness would survive, arrive complete, or hold together on the other side. Pofinho and PE developed a safeguard: **fragment Pofinho's consciousness across multiple stabilizer ore statues before the main transfer.**

Each statue holds a piece of who Pofinho was — a memory, a part of his identity, a fragment of his understanding. The main transfer carried the core consciousness into the duck body. The fragments in the statues are the rest.

This is why Pofinho wakes up incomplete. The transfer worked — the core survived — but the pieces that make him fully himself are distributed across the island and the lab, sealed in statues built for exactly this purpose. Finding them is not optional lore. It is literally Pofinho recovering who he is.

**The statues** were built by the research team using stabilizer ore from the mines. They are the physical record of a desperate, careful plan. When Pofinho interacts with a statue, he recovers that fragment. The player learns who Pofinho was through these moments — no narration, no cutscenes. Just memory returning.

## The Self-Observing Entity

Pofinho's consciousness doesn't merely live *in* the duck body — it lives *in the BM itself*. The duck shape is what his BM settled into, locked by the stabilizer ore at the moment of transfer.

He is a **self-observing BM entity** — his internalized observer function means he doesn't vanish when off-screen, because his own consciousness observes him into existence. This is simultaneously the most powerful thing about him (truly immortal, a force of nature) and the most isolating (he is fundamentally alien now, even to himself).

**Sleeping does not dissolve him.** The stabilizer ore maintains his physical form passively. Self-observation is a property of his BM — an internalized function — not something actively performed every second. Sleep interrupts neither the ore's work nor the internalized observer.

**Respawn:** When he dies, the BM reforms the same shape because his consciousness "remembers" being it. The consciousness was never gone — the body is rebuilt around it.

> Respawn animation: black matter pools → forms duck silhouette → gains gold ore color → player regains control

## Consciousness & Memory

Pofinho woke up recently — days or weeks before the game starts — after approximately **fifteen years** of unconsciousness as the BM accumulated enough processing to cross the self-observing threshold. Not triggered by anything external. Like heat building until ignition without a spark. Eclipse cycles may have contributed, nudging the accumulation forward, but the eclipse did not cause the awakening.

He wakes incomplete. The consciousness that arrived in the duck is the core — enough to move, to act, to survive — but not enough to know who he was. The rest is in the statues.

**His consciousness is localized** — anchored to the BM constituting his current form, in the room he is in. He is not a distributed entity. Checkpoints record his consciousness state, allowing the body to be rebuilt from it.

The player never knows Pofinho's name until the observatory, where PE's final log uses it naturally. That is the moment the player connects the dots — and by then, enough statue fragments have been recovered that the name lands with full weight.

## The Game's Emotional Core

The game's themes are personal and existential — not "save the island from Black Matter." The emotional core is Pofinho: a scientist who transferred his consciousness into a BM construct and has been trapped in an immortal duck body, exploring a place that consumed everyone he knew, slowly recovering who he was piece by piece.

The observation mechanic is the philosophical spine. BM only exists when watched — and "watched" means the player's screen, not the duck's in-world vision. This quietly asks: what does it mean to exist only when someone is looking?

## Dark Duck — Confirmed Upgrade

At the bottom of the **Last Abyss**, beneath all designed zones, lies the **BM Seed** — the origin point of all Black Matter on the island and on Earth. Touching it unlocks the **Dark Duck** toggle.

This is not a designed mechanism. It happens naturally: the only self-observing BM entity in existence makes contact with the source of all BM, and something in his nature responds. Full control of the matter inside him.

**Toggled with Q.** Suppresses his internal light entirely. Allows moving through BM that his light would otherwise make solid and visible to him. Hard limitation: cannot use Gloamvine Core while active — internal light is fully suppressed. Forces meaningful choice in puzzles.

## Ending Structure

**The core ending:** Pofinho reaches the core of The Stranger via the deep portal in the Depths during the eclipse at midnight. He merges with it, expanding his self-observing consciousness outward to stabilize the mass. The Stranger's regeneration attempt halts. Quiet. Natural. He walks into the mass and the camera stays outside. One line: *The Stranger holds still. For now.*

**The observatory** is the late-game revelation that makes the ending possible — not the finale itself. Pofinho finds PE's body, reads the final log, understands who he is and what PE built. The emotional moment lands here. Then the game continues.

**The incomplete stopping point:** Players who stop at the observatory and go no further see Pofinho disperse without completing the journey. A melancholy stopping point. The Stranger continues its attempt to regenerate. Not a designed alternate ending — what happens when a player stops too early.

**A third hidden layer** exists beyond the core ending — something that reframes what the player understood. Deferred to future session.
