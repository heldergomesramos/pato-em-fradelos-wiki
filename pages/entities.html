📍 Chamber of Depths — Walls and Ceilings
Description

Completely two-dimensional creature that exists exclusively on solid surfaces. In its usual state it is totally invisible, blending with the darkness. It has no pursuit behavior nor apparent predatory intent — its danger is purely passive and situational. It's still uncertain if it has its own consciousness or if it's merely an emergent property of dark matter on ancient surfaces.

Behavior States
Rest / Hidden
Completely invisible on surface. Does not react to player or weak light.
Revealed
Intense light from Gloamvine Core reveals its purple outline on the surface.
Drain
When the player enters its radius, instantly absorbs the charge of the nearest Gloamvine Core.

📍 Chamber of Depths — Central Zone
Description

A smaller and less developed form of the same entity that constitutes the Shadow. Presents itself as a concentrated mass of darkness with no limbs, no visible eyes, no defined form. Moves erratically without reacting to the player's presence while emitting light. Its design function is to teach the player that darkness can be used as protection.

States
Wandering
Erratic and random movement. Completely ignores the player.
Retreat
Any light source causes immediate retreat for about one second.
Blind
If the player extinguishes all light, the Wisp passes through them as if they don't exist.
Struck Down
Direct collision with the player while emitting light — instant death.

📍 Chamber of Depths — Final Section
Description

A diffuse entity of pure darkness with no compact physical form. It cannot be blocked, only delayed. Normal light is consumed by it — it pauses briefly to absorb any light source it encounters. This hesitation is the only survival mechanism during pursuit. It dies exclusively on contact with light originating from dark matter — a frequency it cannot process or consume.

Encounter Phases
Phase 1 — Presence
Not visible. Ambient light sources extinguish sequentially in the direction the player came from.
Phase 2 — Chase
Active pursuit. When finding a deployed Core charge, immobilizes for 1–2 seconds to consume it — escape window.
Phase 3 — Final Chamber
Begins consuming ambient light, involuntarily revealing the giant glowvine mechanism.
Death
The light pulse of dark matter from the giant glowvine. The Shadow disintegrates completely.

📍 Aquarium — Main Chamber
Description

Artificially created by the ORBIS Project to simulate the properties of Entities — possesses consciousness to observe and can become 100% black. Perfect spherical form with a single eye. When the eye is closed it is completely invisible in dark matter. Attacks the player for recognizing them as an unauthorized dark matter creature — not catalogued in the system.

States
Rest
Floats slowly with eye open, watching cameras. Ignores player without direct line of sight.
Targeting
Upon detecting the player, begins following them. Vision starts to become slightly reddened.
Irradiation
After 4s of exposure, player's vision completely distorted. Death if line of sight is not broken.
Target Lost
If player hides behind dark matter, Observer returns to rest.

Lore Note

The Observer attacks the duck because it is an unauthorized dark matter creature not catalogued in the system — an intruder. The mini-observers function as cameras controlled by the main Observer. Disabling the Observer disables all mini-observers. It was the first attempt to create an artificial conscious being capable of continuous observation.

📍 All Zones — Regions with Black Matter
Description

Bioluminescent plant that only grows in environments with Black Matter concentration. Its cores emit light originating from dark matter — the only frequency that the Shadow cannot consume. Serves simultaneously as a lighting resource, player projectile, and progression mechanism. Identifiable by visible roots at the flower base — unlike Anglerfish imitations.

Propriedades
Passive Lighting
Illuminates nearby dark matter, revealing hidden creatures in a limited radius.
Collectable Core
Player can harvest the core to use as a projectile. Plant regenerates after some time.
Lurker Revelation
Dark matter light reveals Lurkers on surfaces that would be invisible with normal light.

Design Note

Gloamvine is the central axis of the game's resource system. It manages tension between exploration and combat — the player needs cores to reveal Lurkers, delay the Shadow, and activate mechanisms, but harvesting them requires approaching areas with higher dark matter concentration. Its visual distinction from Anglerfish imitations is the game's first visual literacy test.
