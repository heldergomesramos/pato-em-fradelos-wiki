# Areas — The Stranger

---

## What It Is

A large black planet orbiting between Earth and the Moon, roughly moon-sized. Detected by the research team's instruments — catalogued by humanity as a permanent astronomical feature, strange and studied, but not obviously dangerous. From Earth's surface it looks like it always has. Its size increase is detectable only with the precision instruments PE built at the observatory.

The Stranger is an ancient planet entirely consumed by Black Matter. It has been floating here for thousands of years, slowly dispersing itself. The surface is Black Matter — a scout camera sent to it phased straight through, consistent with a BM surface.

**The Stranger is where unobserved BM goes.** What the scientists called "Internull" is The Stranger's interior. They named it before they understood what they were describing.

→ See `areas/internull.md` for how teleporters and sleep access The Stranger's interior
→ See `mechanics/black_matter.md` for BM origin and the ocean threat

---

## Known Properties

- Causes a **total eclipse every 3 days at midnight** — complete darkness on the island
- A scout camera sent to its surface **phased through it** — surface is BM
- No light reflects off it — absorbs everything, same as BM
- **Growing slowly** — detectable only with PE's precision instruments at the observatory. Not visibly growing to the naked eye. The threat from The Stranger is internal: its interior is destabilizing slowly. From Earth's surface it looks like it always has.

---

## Eclipse Mechanic

Every 3 days at midnight, the island experiences total darkness. During this window:
- All BM on the surface vanishes (unobserved in total darkness) — opens normally-blocked paths
- The Stranger reaches zenith — deepest penetration available for any Stranger zone accessed via sleep + teleporter

The eclipse is one of the two keys to reaching The Stranger's core (the other being the correct coordinates). Eclipse double function: traversal tool mid-game and required for the optional ending.

---

## The Observatory Connection

The observatory sits on the eastern mountain peak. Its telescope points at The Stranger — one of the ways the player pieces together the connection over time. The Black Lake serves as a threshold between mid-game and the final stretch; entering it during the eclipse and exiting through the right side before the moon returns unlocks the path to the observatory.

## Interior — Zone Types

### Void Zones
Near-total darkness. Pofinho's gold glow is the only light source, illuminating a few body-lengths in any direction. BM structures are massive and solid, materializing from pure black as he passes. Frozen creatures are ghosted in cold blue-white at the edges of visibility. Intimate, claustrophobic, tense through not-knowing.

**Palette:** Pure black background, warm gold glow from Pofinho, very faint dark teal edge on BM structures where light catches them, cold blue-white on frozen creatures.

### Radiant Zones
Vast open spaces. The ancient core radiates amber-gold at center, bleeding outward through burnt orange to deep magenta-purple at the edges, fading to black. Platforms and frozen objects are silhouetted against the glow — almost fully black, readable by contrast. Pofinho's own glow is smaller here, competing with the background, emphasizing his scale against the immensity. The vortex rotation is geological — nearly imperceptible moment to moment but clearly different over time.

**Palette:** Amber-gold core bleeding to burnt orange to magenta-purple to black at edges. All platforms and debris are dark silhouettes. Pofinho's gold competes with the background.

The black-and-gold visual language connects to checkpoints, portals, and stabilizer ore throughout the main game — The Stranger feels like the deepest version of something the player has been seeing all along.

---

## Frozen Objects — Visual Treatment

Objects in The Stranger exist in multiple observed states simultaneously — a slight ghosting effect, two or three translucent copies offset by small distances, like a long-exposure photograph of motion. Not paused — preserved mid-motion with the memory of movement visible. Ghost copies are cold blue-white, slightly desaturated.

---

## Pofinho Inside The Stranger

The duck is **black-bodied with gold stabilizer ore trim**. Not yellow. The gold is the ore edges, the bill, the eye — not the body. Inside Void Zones the duck is the warmest thing in the frame by a significant margin. In Radiant Zones the background competes with him.

The key image: duck silhouette — small, warm, moving — against the vast amber vortex.

## Core Gameplay Mechanics (Stranger Zones)

- **Path collapse:** BM surfaces exist only while observed. Moving forward collapses the path behind you. No going back.
- **Sleep as state-collapse:** Sleeping collapses overlapping BM states into one stable nearby configuration — at the cost of drawing something to stir. Use sparingly.
- **Dangerous frozen creatures:** Creatures frozen mid-lunge become real if observed directly. Navigate around them without looking.
- **Eclipse-gated depth:** The Stranger's orbital position (zenith at midnight) controls how deep you can go in any zone.

## The Core

The original BM mass. Ancient, inert, vast. Evidence this process happened to another world long ago and ended. The Stranger *is* that world. It has been floating here the whole game, slowly dispersing itself.

**The ending:** Pofinho — a self-observing BM entity — merges with the core. He expands his self-observing consciousness outward to encompass the mass, stabilizing it. Not a sacrifice cutscene. Quiet. Natural. He walks into the mass and the camera stays outside, watching. The Stranger's growth slows and halts.

One line of text: *The Stranger holds still. For now.*

Then: the island surface, seen from above for the first time. Morning light on the water. Life continuing, unaware. The implication — that someone might still find a better solution someday — is never stated. The player carries it out.

**A third hidden layer** exists beyond this ending — something that reframes what the player understood. Deferred to future session.
