# Areas — The Observatory

> New area added this session. Add to README file map and dependency map.

---

## Location & Geography

Eastern mountain peak — the highest point in the game. The Black Lake sits in an open mountain valley below, exposed to sky. A deep fissure beneath the lake connects underground to the BM generator inside the mountain. The lake is the **reservoir**, the generator is the **engine**, the fissure is the **conduit**.

Vertical order by altitude: lab (lowest) → generator (inside the mountain) → Black Lake (valley) → observatory (peak).

**Unlocking the path:** Enter the Black Lake during the eclipse and exit through the right side before the moon returns. Technically discoverable early, practically reached once the player has enough knowledge to know to try it. The lake is the literal threshold between mid-game and the final stretch.

---

## Why It Exists

Two purposes:

1. **Measure The Stranger's growth rate** — the only instruments sensitive enough to detect its slow size increase. PE's logs document this over decades of readings.
2. **Navigation for the teleporter system** — teleportation routes through The Stranger and the entry point shifts with its orbital position. Exact real-time positional data feeds into portal coordinate calculations. The observatory sphere is the most precisely calibrated portal in the facility.

This is why PE stayed here specifically — the only place with continuous real-time data on The Stranger.

---

## Interior

One large circular room. Telescope through retractable roof aimed at The Stranger. Instruments still running, paper output rolls piled on the floor unread. Cot, cold stove, warm clothing on a hook.

A notebook on the desk — formal logs giving way to cramped personal entries slowing toward the end:

- *"Day 400 or so. Lost count properly."*
- *"The growth rate has been consistent. That's almost worse than acceleration."*
- One late undated entry: *"I think he's close. The pattern on the lake last eclipse — I've seen that before. He's starting to remember the way."*

This last entry reveals PE had been tracking Pofinho's return by reading BM patterns in the Black Lake during eclipses — the same way they tracked The Stranger. Quietly. From a distance. For years.

---

## PE's Fate

PE died before Pofinho arrived. Waited their entire remaining life and didn't make it.

**PE's body** is at the desk, at the telescope, still aimed at The Stranger. They chose where they wanted to be found.

What PE left behind: a final log, completed coordinate calculations, and the deep portal already built. Everything PE could do, they did.

**How PE built the deep portal without entering The Stranger:** built the entry mechanism from the outside. The Depths was chosen because BM concentration there is highest and oldest — the membrane between this world and The Stranger is thinnest there. PE built the door where the wall was already weakest.

---

## Role in the Ending Structure

The observatory is **not the ending**. It is the late-game revelation that makes the ending possible.

Pofinho finds PE's body, reads the final log, understands who he is and what PE built. The emotional moment lands here — before anything else is clear, the log says something personal about Pofinho that only someone who knew him would know. This confirms his identity to the player without narration. Then the game continues to the core.

**The player never knows Pofinho's name until this moment.** PE's final log uses it naturally. That is the moment the player connects the dots.

**The incomplete stopping point:** Players who stop at the observatory and go no further see Pofinho disperse. Not a satisfying conclusion — a melancholy stopping point. The Stranger continues growing. This is what happens when a player stops before finishing, not a designed alternate ending.
